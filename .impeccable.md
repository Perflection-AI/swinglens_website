## Design Context

### Users
Three overlapping visitor types: (1) curious weekend golfers who found the app on social media and need to feel "this is legit" within seconds; (2) skeptical golfers who've tried gimmick apps before and need to feel this is backed by real science; (3) teaching pros evaluating tools for their students, who need to feel the platform is professional-grade. All three are on the same page at the same time.

### Brand Personality
**Precise · Confident · Approachable** — like a really good caddy. Deeply knowledgeable but never condescending. Technically rigorous without being cold. Like having a sports scientist in your pocket who actually loves the game.

### Aesthetic Direction
Reference: Whoop, Arc Browser — clean, data-forward, intentional use of space, strong typography, confident white-space. NOT a generic SaaS startup page. NOT noisy or cluttered.

Light mode. Background: light olive-white (#F8FAF5). Primary green: #719241 (app brand). Gold accent: #A88D37.
Font: DM Sans (matched to app design system — override the reflex font ban here; user's explicit choice).

Anti-references: anything that looks like a generic AI SaaS landing page (purple gradients, glassmorphism everywhere, rainbow gradient text, identical icon-grid feature lists).

### Design Principles
1. **Data-forward confidence** — show numbers, metrics, and outcomes rather than just listing features
2. **Precision over decoration** — every visual element must earn its place; remove anything that's just "design-y"
3. **Consistent brand tokens** — colors, spacing, and type all trace back to the app's own design system
4. **No gradient text, no side-stripe borders** — these are the two biggest AI design tells; absolute ban
5. **Buttons are pills** — all CTAs use `rounded-full` (9999px) per the app design system
